Research

I study the motions of stars and galaxies as a means to explore fundamental physics and cosmology.

Gravity in the Galactic Center

Stars orbitting about the Galactic Center are probes of gravity and general relativity in the vicinity of a supermassive black hole. I study the kinemetics of stellar masers (microwave lasers) in the inner parsec using observations from the Very Large Array (VLA) and Atacama Large Millimeter/submillimeter Array (ALMA). You can read about these observations, the stellar kinematics, and their utility for constraining the mass distribution in the Galactic Center in my latest paper.

Real-time Cosmology

I started my graduate research studying proper motions of galaxies observed by Gaia, which are tracers of cosmic expansion, large scale structure, primordial gravitational waves, and more. Most recently, I developed methods to detect secular extragalactic parallax in future Gaia data releases.

Teaching and Service

It is a great privilege to study astronomy professionally. I believe it is neccessary and personally rewarding to give back to the community through education and service.

Teaching

I was fortunate to have the opportunity to teach ASTR 1000 as instructor of record at University of Colorado during Summer 2020. I wrote the syllabus and developed class materials for remote learning. Since introductory astronomy courses are an excellent gateway to understanding the broader principles of science, I designed unit on scientific literacy for the primarily non-STEM major students taking the class.

Public Outreach

Sharing astronomy with the public is one of the most rewarding aspects of working in this field. I have engaged in outreach since undergrad, where I ran sidewalk astronomy events on my college's campus.
In Boulder, I have educated younger students on topics like black holes, the EM spectrum, and the history of astronomy through volunteering at the Sommers-Bausch Observatory and a local elementary school.

Service

I am very proud to be a part of a department where graduate students have an active and influential role in committees. I have been elected to several committees, including graduate admissions, faculty hiring, comprehensive exams, and graduate cirriculum and concerns. My involvement on these committees and the tireless work of my peers has helped shape department policy and culture.

About me

I am a doctoral candidate and NSF Graduate Research Fellow in the Astrophysics and Planetary Sciences Department at the University of Colorado Boulder. My graduate work with Prof. Jeremy Darling spans extragalactic astrophysics to the Galactic Center, with the common theme of studying the dynamic universe via proper motions. My current focus is the kinematics of stellar SiO masers within the inner parsec of the supermassive black hole Sgr A*.

Before coming to Boulder, I earned a B.S. in Physics from Virginia Tech in 2016. During my undergraduate studies, I worked with Dr. Sara Petty and Prof. Duncan Farrah studying hyperluminous infrared galaxies and quasars. In the summer of 2015, I had the opportunity to attend an NSF REU at the Harvard-Smithsonian Center for Astrophysics, where I worked with Drs. Georgiana Ogrean and Paul Nulsen studying the theromodynamic properties of the galaxy cluster Abell 133.
