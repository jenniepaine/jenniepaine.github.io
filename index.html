Research

I study the motions of stars and galaxies as a means to explore fundamental physics and cosmology.

Gravity in the Galactic Center

Stars orbitting about the Galactic Center are probes of gravity and general relativity in the vicinity of a supermassive black hole. I study the kinemetics of stellar masers (microwave lasers) in the inner parsec using observations from the VLA and ALMA.

Real-time Cosmology

I started my graduate research studying proper motions of galaxies observed by Gaia, which are tracers of cosmic expansion, large scale structure, primordial gravitational waves, and more. Most recently, I developed methods to detect secular extragalactic parallax in future Gaia data releases, which you can read about in my latest paper.

Outreach and Service

It is a great privilege to study astronomy professionally. I believe it is neccessary and personally rewarding to give back to the community through education and service.

Public Outreach

My first outreach experiences were organizing "sidewalk astronomy" observing events as an officer in the Virgina Tech astronomy club. We brought telescopes to campus common areas late at night to educate unsuspecting college students.
As a graduate student, I especially enjoy the opportunities to interact with younger students who always impress me with their creativity and curiousity for science and astronomy. At the Sommers-Bausch Observatory annual Astronomy Day, I have educated elementary students on topics such as black holes and the EM spectrum. I have also volunteered at a Boulder elementary school to teach students about the history of astronomy and our evolving understanding of the cosmos.

Service

I am very proud to be a part of a department where graduate students have an active and influential role in committees. I have been elected to several committees, including graduate admissions, faculty hiring, comprehensive exams, and graduate cirriculum and concerns. My involvement on these committees and the tireless work of my peers has helped shape department policy and culture.

About me

I am a doctoral candidate in the Astrophysics and Planetary Sciences Department at the University of Colorado Boulder. My graduate work with Prof. Jeremy Darling spans extragalactic astrophysics to the Galactic Center, with the common theme of studying the dynamic universe via proper motions. My current focus is the kinematics of stellar SiO masers within the inner parsec of the supermassive black hole Sgr A*.

Before coming to Boulder, I earned a B.S. in Physics from Virginia Tech in 2016. During my undergraduate studies, I worked with Dr. Sara Petty and Prof. Duncan Farrah studying hyperluminous infrared galaxies and quasars. In the summer of 2015, I had the opportunity to attend an NSF REU at the Harvard-Smithsonian Center for Astrophysics, where I worked with Drs. Georgiana Ogrean and Paul Nulsen studying the theromodynamic properties of the galaxy cluster Abell 133.

I live in Boulder with my husband, Richard, an engineer who creates life-saving technology that keeps donor lungs alive before transplant. In my spare time, I enjoy reading with a cat in my lap, cooking, and practicing piano.
